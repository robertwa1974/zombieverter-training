I03-X — HIGH VOLTAGE CONNECTION

The HV BypassThe most expensive mistake in GS450h builds

Two connection points — only one is correct

The GS450h inverter has two places you can connect your HV battery. One is the obvious OEM terminal. The other is a set of brass pillars hidden under a plastic cover. These are not interchangeable.

🔴 OEM Terminals — DO NOT USE

Connecting HV to the OEM positive/negative terminals routes current through the boost inductor and boost IGBT. These components are rated for ~100A maximum and are not designed for continuous EV traction loads. Everything works fine on the bench and at low speed. At highway speed under load — the inverter destroys itself. At least two confirmed inverter failures from this mistake.

✓ Brass Pillars — Correct Connection

Two brass pillars accessed by removing a small black plastic cover and two M6 bolts (10mm head). These connect HV directly to the motor drive stage, bypassing the boost converter entirely. This is the correct connection for EV use.

How to Access the Brass Pillars

1
Locate the black plastic cover
On the inverter body — not the main HV cable entry points. Look for a small rectangular cover near the HV bus.
2
Gently prize off the cover
Toyota uses adhesive. Prize gently — don't crack it. Some builders 3D print a replacement cover.
3
Remove two M6 bolts (10mm head)
Two small bolts secure the brass pillars. Remove them to expose the pillar threads.
4
Connect HV+ to one pillar, HV− to the other
Polarity matters. HV+ to positive pillar, HV− to negative pillar. Secure with appropriate lugs and bolts.
⚠ Bench test tip

For bench testing, a 60V 5A power supply into the brass pillars is sufficient to verify sync serial communication and rotation at low speed. You do not need pack voltage to confirm the system works.

Sync Serial Wiring8 wires — 4 differential pairs — must be shielded twisted

⚠ Polarity matters

Each pair has a + and − wire. Swapping polarity on a pair will prevent communication. Match pin numbers exactly to the inverter connector pinout on the wiki.

Connectivity check (no HV needed)

With 12V on VCU + sync serial wired correctly, check temphs in Spot Values. A reading of ~25–35°C at room temperature confirms the sync serial link is working.

Cable Requirements

Shielded twisted pair
Use screened/shielded cable for each pair. Toyota's OEM harness uses this — building without shielding risks signal corruption and intermittent faults at speed.
Use OEM harness where possible
The "engine wire" harness from a GS450h donor car contains the pre-made, pre-connectorised sync serial cables. Extracting these is far more reliable than building from scratch.

12V Power to Inverter

# OEM harness wire colours (confirm with multimeter) Green ×2 → 12V+ supply to inverter (join both, add fuse) Wht/Blk ×2 → Ground (join both) # 12V supply is switched by VCU relay (Pin 32) # Inverter should NOT power up until VCU is ready
⚠ Start with 1A fuse

Work up to 2A → 3A → 5A if it blows. The GS450h inverter draws more than the VCU at startup — don't assume a 500mA fuse will hold.

⚠ Temp sensor wires must be cut

MG1 and MG2 temperature sensor wires (gray/green on MG2, red/blue on MG1) run inside the resolver cable bundle but do not have through-plugs — they terminate at the "harmonica" junction block. Cut them as far back as possible. They are not used in the ZV integration.

Harness Extraction Tips

1
Identify correct harness: "engine wire"
Look for the plastic "squiggly" guide that runs along the transmission side. Resolver plugs are at the opposite end from the inverter connection — buy harnesses that are not cut at this end.
2
Use a very sharp blade — cut lengthwise
Cut electrical tape and loom lengthwise along the grain. A dull blade will cut into wire insulation. Replace the blade before starting.
3
MG2 plug is rear of transmission, MG1 is front
Label them immediately on extraction. Swapped resolver plugs cause resolver errors and the system will not run correctly.
4
Gen 3 vs Gen 4 plug compatibility
Gen 3 (2007–2011) is the primary supported conversion platform. Gen 4 (2013+) harnesses may work but test first — ZV firmware targets Gen 3 timing.
💡 Buying tip

Harnesses go for ~$125–200 on eBay. Search "GS450h engine wire" or "GS450h transmission harness." Make lowball offers — sellers often accept. Always check photos to confirm the resolver plugs at the far end are not cut.

I03-X — OIL PUMP SYSTEM

The Oil PumpNot optional — the inverter overheats without it

Why the Oil Pump Exists

The GS450h inverter has its own internal oil cooling circuit. In the OEM hybrid vehicle, this pump is driven mechanically. In an EV conversion, there is no engine — so an electric oil pump must run continuously while the inverter is active.

🔴 Run it from precharge. Always.

If the oil pump is not running when the inverter is active, the inverter will overheat. This is not a long-term problem — it's a short-term one. The pump must spin up during precharge and remain on throughout run mode.

Oil Pump Specs

ParameterValue
Current draw~25A from 12V (at full speed)
Power sourceDedicated 12V — NOT from VCU output
Control methodExternal controller with PWM input from VCU
Bench starting point30% PWM duty cycle
Connector typeTwo connectors on controller: power + resolver

Oil Pump Wiring Diagram

# Oil pump system — three wiring connections POWER to controller: 12V+ (battery) ── Blue wire → controller input+ GND (casing) ── Brown wire → controller GND PWM SIGNAL (VCU controls speed): VCU PWM pin ── Black wire → controller PWM input (GS450Hpump function assigned in web UI) PUMP to controller: Oil pump motor ── Power connector on controller Pump resolver ── Resolver connector on controller
⚠ 25A needs its own supply

The oil pump controller draws ~25A from 12V. This cannot come from the VCU output pins — those are low-current signal outputs. Wire the controller power directly from the 12V battery or auxiliary bus with its own fuse (30A minimum).

I03-X — VCU CONFIGURATION

PWM Config & Input Shaft LockCompleting the VCU setup for GS450h

Assigning GS450Hpump in the VCU

1
In web interface → Parameters → Dout
Find the configurable PWM output pins section
2
Assign GS450Hpump to the Pump PWM pin
This is a high-side PWM output. In firmware V2.20A+, PumpPWM is a selectable output function — no longer hardcoded to GS450h builds only.
3
Start at 30% PWM duty cycle
Damien's bench setup runs at 30%. This is sufficient for low-load operation. Increase for sustained high-load or warm ambient temperatures.
4
Verify pump is spinning before applying HV
You should hear the pump. Check during precharge — if the pump is not audible, fix it before proceeding.
V2.20A note

Before V2.20A, the GS450h oil pump output was hardcoded. From V2.20A onwards, PumpPWM is a freely assignable output function — useful for builds that want coolant pump PWM control on non-GS450h systems too.

Input Shaft Lock — The Critical Mod

Without locking the input shaft, MG1 can only spin the engine input shaft — it cannot send torque to the output. The lock forces MG1 torque through the power split device to the output shaft, giving you both motors driving the wheels.

🔴 Without the lock

MG1 provides no useful traction — it just spins freely. You lose roughly half your available torque and power. This is a mechanical modification, not a software setting.

What the lock looks like

A plate or bar with a spline fitting that bolts to the bellhousing flange, engaging the engine input shaft splines and preventing rotation. For bench testing: Damien uses a vice grip and cable tie. For a real vehicle: a purpose-made billet lock plate.

⚠ MG1 torque ratio

With the shaft locked, MG1 torque is geared up to MG2 at approximately 2.6–2.7:1. This means MG1 effectively contributes more torque at the output than its nameplate rating suggests — it is a significant contribution, not a minor addition.
